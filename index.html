Connecting school districts with affordable, quality textbooks

TextParade is a nonprofit that partners with textbook creators and schools to bring high-quality, affordable textbooks students, breaking the cycle of expensive educational materials.

Our Mission

Quality Without Compromise

We believe every student deserves access to well-researched, expertly-written educational materials. By partnering with peer-reviewed, free-to-use textbooks, we take no profit and focus on keeping prices as low as possible for schools.

Affordable Access

By working directly with publishers and schools, we eliminate unnecessary markup and deliver textbooks at prices that public school districts at a fraction of the cost of larger publishers.

Sustainable Solutions

Through grant procurement, market research, and strategic outreach, we build lasting partnerships that ensure continued access to quality educational resources.

Implementation Expertise

We understand the complexities of textbook deployment in public school systems. Our team is well-versed in educational policy, procurement codes, and administrative requirements, allowing us to streamline adoption processes and navigate bureaucratic challenges efficiently.

About Us

TextParade was founded by Xander Mitchell and Noah Lee, two passionate Chicago-based student educators and college history majors. We started this project with the deep belief that physical textbooks generate stronger learning outcomes.

Textbooks are today both expensive and unavailable. Their rapid increase in price has completely outpaced the CPI for other goods and for that reason and others, the purchasing of new textbooks has become prohibitively expensive for school districts regardless of textbook benefits.

Even though both educators and students recognize the value in textbooks—such as less eye strain, reliability, and information retention—districts and individual schools are pushing teachers to develop independent curriculums in part to skip out on this cost. As textbook prices rises and the shift to digital resources continues, we are finding solutions to ensure textbooks have a place in the classroom.

Our collaboration with Stanford University Press's American Yawp textbook pairs excellent American History content with an on-the-ground nonprofit mission. This partnership allows us to offer American history textbooks that meet the highest scholarly standards while remaining accessible to public school budgets.

Through this alliance, we leverage Stanford's rigorous research and peer review standards, ensuring that students receive educational materials that are both accurate and engaging.
